Ahmed Maksud, Ph.D.

Wireless systems researcher — IEEE 802.11 MAC · ML/RL for Wi-Fi · PHY-layer security & DSP

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Texas State University, building intelligent and sustainable next-generation Wi-Fi: reinforcement-learning-based Target Wake Time (TWT) scheduling and RF energy harvesting for IEEE 802.11 networks, implemented as protocol-compliant, open-source ns-3 pipelines (C++/Python). I received my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from UC Riverside (2024), where I worked on physical-layer security — continuous encryption functions, MIMO secret-key capacity, and OFDM secrecy optimization — bridging formal analysis (information theory, random-matrix theory) with reproducible implementation.
